Lesson 1

Ideation

Today is about building the foundation of your app. Before we touch a single line of code or design element, we have to establish the "Why."

This lesson is not about design — it is about purpose. We need to define exactly what we are building and the specific problem it solves.

Research & Insight

Topic 1
When starting a project, your research determines your ceiling.
  • The AI Limit: ChatGPT is a powerful tool, but it often provides surface-level information. It can summarize known facts, but it lacks the nuance of real-world application.
  • The Power of Experience: Experience is your best teacher. If you are relatively new, you need to borrow the experience of others.
  • Deep Dives via YouTube: YouTube is an incredible resource for seeing exactly how systems work in the wild. Ask ChatGPT to find specific tutorials or case studies related to the workflow you are researching.

The MVP

Topic 2
MVP

Function Before Form

In development, we often get distracted by "shiny object syndrome." We want every feature at once.

  • The MVP is the most basic version of your product that still solves the core problem.
  • Our goal is to ensure the app serves its primary function first.
  • We prioritize functionality now and focus on making it beautiful later.
  • If the core logic doesn't work, a pretty interface won't save it.

The Prompt

Topic 3
Google Stitch

Talking to the AI

Once we know the purpose and the MVP, we need to translate that into a language the AI understands. We will create a master prompt that tells Google Stitch exactly how to build our design.

  • Your first draft can be messy. Do not worry about perfect grammar yet.
  • To get your ideas out faster, consider using Wispr Flow or iPhone Voice Notes.
  • Simply record your thoughts and copy/paste the transcript.

Core Method

What are the pros and cons of the current system? If no system exists, what makes ours unique?

What are the "must-have" features for the MVP?

5

The Vibe

This is your opportunity to say any details that are just in your head that you want to see — while still keeping it fundamentally simple.
